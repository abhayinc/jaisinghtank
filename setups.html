Episodic Pivot (EP)
Institutional Re-Rating

The Episodic Pivot (EP) is the ultimate setup for capturing massive institutional re-rating. In the Indian market, these often occur in sectors with high structural tailwinds—like Defence, Tech, and Consumer Retail. Here are three classic examples.

EP Anatomy (Gap Up Strategy)
Case Study // NSE:MAZDOCK
Mazagon Dock Shipbuilders

THE CATALYST

A massive structural shift in India's defence spending. MAZDOCK provided a clean EP on the back of multi-thousand crore order wins for submarines and destroyers. Institutions were forced to revalue the entire company overnight due to the multi-year revenue visibility.

GAP UP
7% - 12%
VOLUME
10X AVG
Case Study // NSE:ZOMATO
Zomato Ltd (Blinkit Growth)

THE CATALYST

The "Quick Commerce" revolution. Zomato's Q4 FY26 results shattered expectations, primarily driven by Blinkit's explosive growth and profitability. This was a classic "New High" Episodic Pivot that signaled a fundamental shift from a loss-making startup to a profitable cash-flow machine.

GAP UP
5% - 8%
VOLUME
8X AVG
Case Study // NSE:TRENT
Trent Ltd (Tata Retail)

THE CATALYST

Consistent "Super-Normal" growth in Westside and Zudio brands. Trent has provided multiple EPs, but the Feb 8, 2024 move (26.6% reaction) stands as one of the greatest examples of the market realizing the sheer scale of the retail dominance being built by the Tata Group.

GAP UP
10% - 25%
VOLUME
15X AVG

The Execution: Trading the Indian EP

Wait for the 15-Minute Opening Range Breakout (ORB). Buy as the price clears the high of the first 15 minutes. Stop loss goes at the day's low or the gap-up level. In the Indian market, these often lead to multi-month trends. Do not fight institutional volume.

Bull Flag / Continuation
15 High-Conviction Examples

The Bull Flag is a powerful continuation pattern. After a strong vertical rally (the pole), the stock takes a breather in a tight, orderly consolidation (the flag). The breakout through the flag's resistance often leads to another move equal to the size of the pole.

The Strategy: Buy the breakout above the high of the flag. The tight consolidation reduces risk and allows for a very close stop-loss just below the flag's base.

// Rare & Explosive
High Tight Flag (HTF)
The Most Powerful Pattern

The High Tight Flag (HTF) is the rarest and most explosive setup. It requires a 100% move in 4–8 weeks, followed by a tight consolidation (less than 20% correction) for 3–5 weeks. This indicates massive institutional accumulation that hasn't finished.

Criteria: 100%+ move first. Then a very tight flag. If it corrects more than 25%, it's likely not an HTF but a regular bull flag. Accuracy is key here.
